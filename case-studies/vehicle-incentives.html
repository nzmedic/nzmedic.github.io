Case study: Vehicle Introducer Incentives

How scenario analysis was used to balance growth, credit risk, and incentive cost — with governance guardrails.

Open the cockpit All cockpits

Decision

Whether to adjust introducer incentives to increase originations without breaching risk appetite or eroding margin.

Stakeholders and incentives

  • Exec sponsor: wants profitable growth with clear downside protection.
  • Analytics lead: needs a pragmatic tool to support decisions and monitoring.
  • Credit/risk: prioritises guardrails, early warning indicators, and review cadence.
  • CTO/founder mindset: wants lightweight delivery, fast iteration, and clear value.
  • Sales/channel: wants incentives that change behaviour without creating adverse selection.

Approach

The work was structured to make trade-offs explicit and testable:

  1. Translate the commercial question into a small set of decision levers.
  2. Quantify outcomes under uncertainty (expected value + downside).
  3. Define guardrails aligned to risk appetite.
  4. Package it as an interactive “cockpit” for stakeholder conversations.

What the cockpit shows

  • Profit distribution: not just the average, but the downside.
  • Trade-off curve: how profit changes as risk (loss-rate delta) increases.
  • Decision summary: an exec-friendly interpretation of the scenario.
  • Guardrails: simple policy thresholds that flip from “OK” to “Breach”.

Governance and monitoring plan

The output of the analysis wasn’t “a model” — it was a decision plus an operating approach:

  • Define success metrics (originations uplift, margin, arrears/loss early indicators).
  • Set guardrails (probability of loss, downside profit threshold, loss-rate drift).
  • Agree review cadence (weekly for pilot, then monthly).
  • Predefine actions when guardrails breach (pause, tighten criteria, adjust incentives).

Outcome

A decision-ready artefact that supports an exec conversation, aligns stakeholders, and can be reused for ongoing governance. The initial version is intentionally lightweight; future iterations can swap in the full Monte Carlo engine and richer data.

Next iteration (v2)

  • Export a one-page “board slide” summary per scenario.
